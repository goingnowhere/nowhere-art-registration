Safety

The local authorities require installations to comply with some safety regulations, so we have to inform you about them and ask you to agree to take responsibility for being compliant.

Please be aware that in theory inspectors might come and ask you to modify or remove your installation if it does not respect these regulations (though in practice this rarely happens). We suggest that you pay very close attention to the following points.

If you have any questions please email us and we will do our best to help you.

It is required that you make the edges of your installation clearly visible at night. This is a safety measure to prevent art cars and people to smash into your art in the darkness. Small LED lights at the corners will suffice for small structures, while larger structures will require perimeter light stips or spotlights

All surfaces higher than 1.5 meters where a person might stand are required to have solid barriers or railings preventing people from falling off. If you can not guarantee this, you should try your best to prevent access to such surfaces.

The region in which we hold the festival is at extreme risk of fires in summer. Because of this a fire ban is in effect. Any kind of solid fuel (wood, coal, etc.) and anything that creates sparks when burning is completely forbidden. Gas and liquid fuel flames are allowed at a safe distance from other structures and from the vegetation at the edges of the site.

Funding

Please check the box above if you would like to request Nowhere to provide you money to buy the materials or services needed to create your art. We can also help funding shipment of your art and/or of your equipment (but we will not contribute to your personal travel costs).

We can only fund up to 70% of the total cost of your project and upon receiving a copy of the expense receipts after the event.

Please remember that Nowhere is entirely volunteer run, and everyone buys their ticket, including artists.

We will use the information in the following sections to decide whether or not to finance your art, and how much money to assign to you.

We appreciate honesty: if we are not convinced by something you write and we are interested in your art, we will contact you and ask for more explanations, and work with you to resolve any issue that might prevent you from getting funded.

How much do you expect to spend in total to create your art ? If you don't know for sure please provide your best estimate.

How much money would you like to request ? Please keep in mind that this can not be more than 70% of the total cost of your art (i.e. what you entered in the field above)

Feel free to leave blank if you are requesting an amount close to the full cost.

Please give us more details about what you are going to buy with the funds, and what the estimated costs of each of these items/services are. You do not need to account for every screw or piece of fabric, but at least give us a reasonable idea of what we will find in the invoices that you will send us.

Please describe to us what is your general plan, from now until the start of the event, to successfully create and deliver your art. You don’t have to give us all the details: just a general overview will be enough. The more reasonable and realistic the plan is, the more likely it is that we will allocate more funds to you.

If your receive less funding than expected, are you planning to scale down your project instead of abandoning it ? If unforseen problems prevent you from being able to complete your main plan, do you have a "plan B" ? This is optional, but we do feel more comfortable funding art with a solid backup plan.
